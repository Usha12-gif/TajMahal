Chronological History

Trace the timeless legacy of the monument, from its royal conception to its modern-day global heritage status.

1631

The Tragic Catalyst

Empress Mumtaz Mahal passes away during childbirth. Grieved by her death, Emperor Shah Jahan vows to construct an unmatched grand mausoleum in her memory.

1632

Construction Begins

Groundwork starts along the Yamuna River banks in Agra. Over 20,000 artisans, architects, and stone carvers are gathered across Asia to work on the complex design.

1643

The Tomb Completion

The central white marble dome structure is largely completed, while work continues on surrounding structures like the mosque, guest house, and iconic minarets.

1983

UNESCO World Heritage

The Taj Mahal is designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized globally as a masterpiece of human creative genius and Muslim art in India.
